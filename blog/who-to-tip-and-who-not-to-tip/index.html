Who to Tip and Who Not to Tip in the US

A clear, no-guilt guide to who needs a tip and who is completely fine without one

Tip prompts now appear at coffee kiosks, self-checkout screens, and food trucks where you hand your own bag over the counter. Not all of them reflect a genuine social expectation. Here's who actually expects a tip — and who is completely fine without one.

Tip These People

Restaurants and Bars

  • Sit-down restaurant server: Yes, always — 18%–20%
  • Bartender (table service): Yes — included in restaurant tip
  • Bartender (bar only): $1–$2 per drink, or 15%–20% of tab
  • Food runner: Included in server tip (pooled)
  • Busser: Included in server tip (pooled)

Personal Care

  • Hair stylist, colorist, barber: Yes — 15%–20%. Calculator
  • Nail technician: Yes — 15%–20%
  • Massage therapist: Yes — 15%–20%
  • Tattoo artist: Yes — 15%–20%. Calculator
  • Esthetician / waxing: Yes — 15%–20%

Delivery and Transportation

  • Food delivery (DoorDash, Uber Eats): Yes — 15%–20%, $3 minimum. Calculator
  • Grocery delivery (Instacart): Yes — 10%–15%. Calculator
  • Rideshare (Uber, Lyft): Yes — 15%–20%. Calculator
  • Valet: Yes — $3–$5 at pickup. Calculator
  • Movers: Yes — $20–$50 per person. Calculator
  • Furniture delivery: Yes — $5–$20 per person. Calculator

Hotels and Travel

  • Hotel housekeeper: Yes — $2–$5 per night, daily. Calculator
  • Bellhop: Yes — $1–$2 per bag. Calculator
  • Concierge (special help): Yes — $5–$20 depending on task
  • Hotel shuttle driver: $2–$3 per person

Don't Tip These People

  • Fast food workers: Counter service, you pick up your own food — not expected
  • Coffee shop counter (standard): Optional — the jar is there but the expectation isn't strong
  • Retail store employees: No
  • Mechanics: No — they charge a shop rate that includes their labor
  • Plumbers, electricians, HVAC: No — set professional rates
  • Doctors, dentists, therapists: No — and it can feel awkward
  • Lawyers and accountants: No — send a referral instead
  • Government employees: Often illegal (post office, DMV, etc.)
  • Flight attendants: Generally no — though some accept and appreciate it
  • Self-checkout or kiosk prompts: Never obligated — these are generated by software

The Gray Zone

  • Dog groomer: Yes — especially mobile or independent. Calculator
  • House cleaner: Yes for regular cleaners; optional for one-time. Calculator
  • Power washer / landscaper: Optional, appreciated — not required
  • Personal trainer: Optional for regular trainers; tip a session's value at holidays
  • Uber Black / premium car service: 20% — same as rideshare
