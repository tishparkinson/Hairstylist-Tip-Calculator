Do You Tip a House Painting Crew?

Full crews need a different approach than a solo painter — here is how

Tipping painters is not a standard expectation in the US — painters are tradespeople who typically charge a set rate that already accounts for their skill and labor. That said, tipping is increasingly appreciated when the work is exceptional, the conditions were difficult, or you want to acknowledge a crew that went above and beyond.

Is Tipping Painters Expected?

No — not in the way tipping a restaurant server is expected. Painting contractors set professional rates and do not rely on tips as a portion of their income. Most painters will be genuinely surprised and appreciative if you tip. A glowing online review, a referral to a neighbor, and cold drinks on a hot day are equally meaningful gestures.

When Tipping Makes Sense

  • The crew worked in extreme heat or difficult conditions
  • They completed the job ahead of schedule with exceptional quality
  • They moved furniture, protected your belongings carefully, and cleaned up thoroughly
  • It's a solo painter or small independent operator who gave you a fair price
  • The project was more complex or time-consuming than originally scoped

Cash or Check?

Cash handed directly to each painter at the end of the job ensures every person receives their share. If it's a larger crew managed by a foreman, you can give the foreman a total amount and ask them to distribute it — but individual cash is always the more direct and appreciated approach.

Alternatives to a Cash Tip

If tipping feels awkward for a large contractor job, consider: a cold lunch on a long day, beverages in hot weather, a detailed 5-star online review (which genuinely helps their business), or a referral to a neighbor or friend. Small business painting contractors value these as much or more than a cash tip.
