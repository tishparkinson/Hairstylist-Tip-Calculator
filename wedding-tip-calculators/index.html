💌 How to Prepare Wedding Vendor Tip Envelopes

The most organized approach is to prepare individual tip envelopes before your wedding day with each vendor's name and the cash amount already inside. Hand the envelopes to your wedding planner, maid of honor, or best man with a list of who receives each one.

This ensures you don't need to handle cash or think about tips on your wedding day. Time each distribution for the end of the vendor's service — after the ceremony for officiants, end of reception for bartenders and DJs, before photographers leave for the night.

If you need to write a check for a larger vendor tip, do so in advance and include it with a thank-you card. For vendors who prefer digital tips, Venmo or Zelle at the end of the event is perfectly acceptable.

Frequently Asked Questions

The most common questions about tipping wedding vendors in the United States.

How much should I budget for wedding vendor tips?

Budget 5%–15% of your total vendor spend. For a $30,000 wedding, that's $1,500–$4,500 across all vendors. Prioritize the vendors who provided the most personal, hands-on service — coordinators, hair and makeup, photographers — over vendors with included service charges.

When do you tip wedding vendors?

At the end of each vendor's service. Have tip envelopes prepared in advance. Delegate distribution to your coordinator, MOH, or best man so you can be present with your guests instead of managing logistics.

Do you tip vendors who own their own business?

Yes — the old etiquette that you don't tip business owners is widely considered outdated. Self-employed photographers, DJs, florists, and coordinators appreciate tips just as much as employees. A tip is a direct acknowledgment of exceptional work, not just a wage supplement.

What if gratuity is already included in the contract?

Check whether the service charge goes to the individual workers or to the company. If it goes to the company, tipping the crew directly is still meaningful. If it goes to the staff, an additional tip is optional but welcome for exceptional service.

Is cash the best way to tip wedding vendors?

Generally yes — cash is immediate, private, and goes directly to the vendor with no processing fees. For digital-native vendors (younger photographers, DJs), Venmo or Zelle at the end of the event is also widely accepted and appreciated.

How much to tip wedding vendors for a destination wedding?

Destination wedding vendors who travel overnight or internationally deserve a higher tip — 15%–20% of their fee is appropriate regardless of whether they charged a travel fee. The additional commitment of destination work goes well beyond what the travel expense covers.

Do you tip a videographer at a wedding?

Yes — video tips follow the same norms as photography. 10%–15% of the videography fee is standard, with 15%–20% for exceptional work or a destination assignment. Tip a second camera operator separately ($50–$150).
